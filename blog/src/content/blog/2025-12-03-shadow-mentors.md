### **The Ultimate Recipe for Professional Self-Sabotage**
**Yields:** One very confused, chronically "almost" human

#### **Ingredients:**
- **1 cup of "I’ll start tomorrow" syndrome** (freshly procrastinated)
- **½ cup of "I’m not good enough" self-doubt** (organic, no additives)
- **1 tbsp of "comparisonitis"** (imported from Instagram)
- **¼ cup of "I’ll do it when I’m ready"** (read: never)
- **1 dash of "what if I fail?"** (the secret spice)
- **½ cup of "I’ll just check my phone one more time"** (the modern-day distraction)
- **1 pinch of "I deserve this"** (for the comfort zone, because comfort is a *right*)
- **1 ghost of your potential** (homemade, from your own "almost" achievements)
- **A splash of "I’ll just Google it"** (the ultimate cop-out)

#### **Instructions:**

1. **Preheat your life to "lukewarm" (or "I’ll figure it out later")**
   - Turn on the oven (your brain) to the lowest setting. The Shadow Mentors thrive in environments where the temperature is just right for *inaction*. No need for high heat—this isn’t a gourmet dish, just a *comfortable* one.

2. **Mix the "I’ll start tomorrow" syndrome with the "I’m not good enough" self-doubt**
   - In a large bowl (your mind), combine the two ingredients until they form a thick, gloopy paste. This is the foundation of your *motivation*. Stir vigorously while whispering, *"I’ll do it when I’m ready… which is never."*

3. **Add the "comparisonitis" and let it simmer**
   - Dump in the comparisonitis and let it bubble for 24 hours. The Shadow Mentors *love* this step—it’s how they turn your feed into a highlight reel of other people’s lives while hiding the *real* story (which is usually "I faked it till I made it… and then quit").

4. **Fold in the "I’ll do it when I’m ready" (read: never) and the "what if I fail?" spice**
   - Gently fold these in, being careful not to overmix. The "what if I fail?" spice is *essential*—it’s what gives your life its signature flavor of *paralysis*. Taste as you go. If it tastes like regret, you’re doing it right.

5. **Add the "I’ll just check my phone one more time" distraction**
   - This is the *secret weapon* of the Shadow Mentors. Every time you reach for your phone, imagine it as a ladle stirring your life’s pot. The more you check, the more your "project" (or life) stays in a *perfectly* unfinished state.

6. **Bake at 37°C (98.6°F) for "forever" (or until you hit snooze 10 times)**
   - Pop your life into the oven and set the timer for *never*. The Shadow Mentors prefer their dishes *rare*—just warm enough to be comfortable, but never quite cooked.

7. **Garnish with the "I deserve this" comfort zone**
   - Sprinkle generously over the top. This is the *final touch* that makes your life *satisfying* (in the way a bowl of ice cream is satisfying after a breakup).

8. **Serve immediately with a side of "I’ll just Google it"**
   - Plate your life and garnish with the ultimate cop-out. The Shadow Mentors *adore* this—it’s how they keep you from ever *actually* doing anything. *"I’ll just Google it"* is the modern-day equivalent of *"I’ll think about it tomorrow."*

9. **Let it rest in the fridge of "I’ll deal with it later"**
   - Store your life in the fridge of procrastination. The Shadow Mentors *love* cold, unappetizing dishes. The longer it sits, the more it tastes like *regret*.
